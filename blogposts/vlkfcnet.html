It was nearing the end of my junior year. For the last 4 weeks of my Machine Learning class, we each worked on independent projects of our choice. This was the project I decided to take on: to program a one layer, fully connected neural net with softmax loss and stochastic gradient descent trained on MNIST on the GPU, from scratch.

Theoretically it should have been very simple: it's only one layer, no frills, and I'm training it on the simplest data set out there. It wouldn't perform well or be anything but a proof-of-concept exercise, but it *seemed* like it was the right scope of project.

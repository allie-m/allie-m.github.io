Taking a cue from my friend Jacob, I've decided to concisely review some books I've read (or listened to). Read his book reviews, they're good.

My reviews are not restricted to books I've read in a particular year, and I am allowed write more than one sentence. This is not comprehensive, and I will probably update it in the future. Expect more content!!

All The Young Dudes

In my 10th grade year, I and most of my grade read this book together. The seminal Harry Potter fanfiction that spawned the Marauders fandom, this 500k word experience is at once heartbreaking, sweet, depressing, beautiful, it is a slow burn and an emotional rollercoaster that made us all cry at least once.

Bobiverse Series

Delightfully self-indulgent, this series is jam-packed with wish fulfillment and nerd references, it is fluffy and fun. It is not the most substantial, coherently worldbuilt, or conceptually interesting sci-fi you'll ever read, but I had a blast listening to it (the audiobooks are better than the written ones) and eagerly await the next sequel.

The Expanse Series

Serious and acutely sensitive, this science fiction series does worldbuilding well, and character drama just as well. It was a captivating, if sometimes heavy read.

The Years of Rice and Salt

I didn't think it was possible to gracefully span a plot across 7 centuries, but Kim Stanley Robinson did it! Some of the constituent stories were more interesting than others (Awake to Emptiness is the most entertaining by a mile), but I was always enthralled trying to piece together the development of the world. The audiobook is sometimes a bit too fast, but I like the narrator he's very emotive. Also the fact one of the characters becomes a professor at what is basically UC Davis is very funny to me.

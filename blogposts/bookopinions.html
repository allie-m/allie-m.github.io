Taking a cue from my friend Jacob, I've decided to concisely review some books I've read (or listened to). Read his book reviews, they're good.

My reviews are not restricted to books I've read in a particular year, and I am allowed write more than one sentence. This is not comprehensive, and I will probably update it in the future. Expect more content!!

I am an audiobook person, so most of these reviews are from the perspective of a listener; whether I listened to or read a book is marked.

All The Young Dudes (Text)

In my 10th grade year, I and most of my grade read this book together. The seminal Harry Potter fanfiction that spawned the Marauders fandom, this 500k word experience is at once heartbreaking, sweet, depressing, beautiful, it is a slow burn and an emotional rollercoaster that made us all cry at least once.

Bobiverse Series (Audio)

Delightfully self-indulgent, this series is jam-packed with wish fulfillment and nerd references, it is fluffy and fun. It's not the most thoughtful sci-fi you'll ever encounter, but I had a blast listening to it, and eagerly await the next sequel.

Grant (Audio)

Slowly paced and sometimes a little dry, I still must recommend this seminal biography of a much maligned and underrated American hero -- he has a pretty interesting story!

Firefly Series (Audio)

Firefly having been cut criminally short and Serenity postfixed to give some closure, this book series extends the Firefly universe with infixed episodic adventures that perfectly complement the main canon. If you love Firefly, listen to these books (in order, ideally), and they will scratch that itch.

The Expanse Series (Audio)

Serious and acutely sensitive, this science fiction series worldbuilds brilliantly, painting a vivid and eminently human picture, and handles characters with the same deft touch. It was a captivating, if sometimes heavy read.

The Idiot (Text)

A Jacob recommendation, I found this book boring on first read and gave up a third of the way through. On my second read, though, I must have changed headspaces because the book's lowkey observational humor was suddenly 100x more funny.

The Years of Rice and Salt (Audio & Text)

I didn't think it was possible to gracefully span a plot across 7 centuries, but Kim Stanley Robinson did it! Some of the constituent stories were more interesting than others (Awake to Emptiness is the most entertaining by a mile), but I was always enthralled trying to piece the bigger picture together. The audiobook is sometimes a bit too fast, and it's easier to catch details in the text version, but I like the narrator.

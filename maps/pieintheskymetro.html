Ok, maybe it's not the *quite* best timeline if you live in the northern parts of SF, I probably should have built them a metro line...

Still, I'm pretty happy with this map, I think it has very reasonable service patterns and routing, and it integrates intercity service coherently -- see the Best Timeline West Coast Intercity Trains map for context there.

My friend is agitating for another transbay tube so that the Bay Area's main intercity rail hub isn't in Fruitvale. I think both a transbay bridge and tube is plenty, personally,

This map also has trains running on the bottom deck of the Golden Gate Bridge, so the revived intercity line to Eureka can (after attaching the cars to an EMU) run to the Transbay Terminal.

Several streetcar rights of way are converted to grade separated metro, most notably the Key System's E Claremont, F Downtown Berkeley, and H Sacramento, the East Bay Electric's Bancroft line, and Muni's M Oceanview and N Judah.

Unlike the current BART setup, Downtown and North Berkeley are served by a different line than what serves Richmond and El Cerrito. That line runs up the H's right of way and then joins the current BART right of way in Westbrae. No veering west at Downtown Berkeley.

The old Southern Pacific line running through what is now prime Silicon Valley land is kept for commuter rail use.

A lot of the "how" in this lore is up to you (maybe California was atypically forward thinking), but it is also definitively an alternate history. Maybe someday I'll do an alternate future...

Oh also a Treasure Island based airport (Harvey Milk International Airport!!) makes a return. I feel like transbay routes going through Yerba Buena island just necessitate an airport there, yk.

During the summer of 2022, I created an entry for the second Summer of Math Exposition, a math explainer contest created by 3Blue1Brown. The video is available on Youtube.

As a math explainer my video is not especially good, but I was glad to get all my thoughts about this math off of my chest.

In spring 2020, a few weeks after quarantine began, I took the last math class of 8th grade, and my favorite math class to date. It was a Number Theory class going over math related to fractions and rational approximations: Farey Sequences, Ford Circles, the Stern-Brocot tree, and Conway's Topograph.

The Stern-Brocot tree's enumeration of the rationals made me curious: could it be useful as a representation of rationals, the way floating point numbers are? Can arithmetic be done on the tree?

This sent me down the long rabbithole that led to this video, as well as another project to get continued logarithm based arithmetic running on the GPU.

Thank you, Dr. Shapiro, for the hard-hitting number theory class. It has stuck with me more than any other math class I've taken.

In the last few weeks of my 11th grade machine learning class, we had the opportunity to pursue independent projects. I decided to make a simple neural net on the GPU from scratch: a fully connected, one layer net with a softmax loss function, training on MNIST.

The code is available on GitHub.

In hindsight, I should have just learned OpenCL, would've taken less time than what I did. I initially used wgpu until I found it didn't have support for floating point atomics at which point I rewrote it all in Vulkan, which had access to floating point atomics on my GPU (after trying unsuccessfully to hack support into wgpu).

It took a very long time and was a meandering and frustrating journey that ended with a functioning but not very good neural net barely a smidge faster than a CPU implementation I slapped together in 15 minutes. I was reminded of why I don't use Vulkan for most things.

Not a great result, and my code is really terrible, but it has made me more comfortable writing (and also wary of) Vulkan.
